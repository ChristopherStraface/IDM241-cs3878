OMED 8013S Research/Scholarship*** 0.0 Credits

This 4-week clerkship provides third-year medical students with dedicated time to engage in (1) research projects across a diverse spectrum of fields, including but not limited to basic science, clinical, translational, medical education, business of health care, and public health research; (2) scholarship activities pertaining to community focused projects and activities; or (3) leadership development activities. Students may continue prior projects or embark on new endeavors, focusing on research, community service, or leadership initiatives. The course will culminate in a tangible scholarly product that reflects student work, such as a manuscript, presentation, program proposal, or other significant output.

OMED 8016S Clerkship Fundamentals*** 0.0 Credits

This six-week course serves as an introduction to the clinical clerkships and seeks to equip students with the basic skills needed to succeed in the clinical years. This course will reinforce clinical skills including history-taking, note-writing and physical exam, as well as introduce broader topics relevant to modern clinical practice, such as usage of evidence-based medicine, management of psychosocial aspects of patient care, and practical approaches to common clinical conditions. This course will also address topics unique to being a clerkship student, including the need to balance studying, participation in clinical care, and emotional well-being.

OMED 8432S Advanced Trial Advocacy- Law and Medicine 0.0 Credits

This course will consist of 7 weeks of 2-hour substantive demonstration/performance classes that will take place from 6-8pm on Tuesdays. ATA Law and Medicine utilizes a mixed classroom model bringing together medical students and law students to facilitate analysis of medical malpractice cases from both the legal and medical perspectives. In the 2-hour class, students will learn how to analyze medical malpractice cases and prepare medical witnesses for depositions and trial. The topics of the course are based on advanced trial advocacy tactics, including opening and closing statements, direct, cross and redirect examination of lay and expert witnesses, and witness preparation.

OMED 8644S Proj in Med Ed: Curriculum Review 0.0 Credits

This course will allow students the opportunity to review medical school curriculum through a lens of bias. Students will participate in a training session to understand types of bias and how to use the SUNY Upstate Bias Checklist. Additional training will be done through a facilitated review with feedback. Students will then be assigned to review specific curricular content using the checklist. Each student will have a faculty advisor throughout the rotation. At the conclusion of the rotation, students will present their findings (trends in bias; recs to remediate bias, etc.) to the group.

Writing-intensive Requirements

In order to graduate, all students must pass three writing-intensive courses after their freshman year. Two writing-intensive courses must be in a student's major. The third can be in any discipline. Students are advised to take one writing-intensive class each year, beginning with the sophomore year, and to avoid “clustering” these courses near the end of their matriculation. Transfer students need to meet with an academic advisor to review the number of writing-intensive courses required to graduate.

For additional information, and an up-to-date list of the writing-intensive courses being offered, students should check the Drexel University Writing Center page
